Wildlife Management Areas, Deer Quality, and Rural Land Values: A Spatial Hedonic Analysis of Arkansas

Tandem Young

April 2026 — Working Draft

Develops and validates a spatial hedonic pipeline for estimating the proximity, trophy-quality, and wildlife-disease effects of public hunting lands on rural land values in Arkansas. Uses 308,220 tax-assessed agricultural parcels as a proof-of-concept source, matched to seventeen spatial control variables spanning soil quality, hydrology, land cover, roads, urban access, federal public lands, gas wells, CRP, elevation, deer-management zones, CWD zones, and AGFC harvest biodata. Econometric properties are demonstrated through nine identification-robustness perturbations. The WMA < 1/4 mile coefficient is stable at approximately −0.19 across all perturbations, with a placebo distribution confirming the effect is specific to actual WMA locations (p < 0.001). The validated pipeline is designed to detect the theoretically predicted positive gradient in sale data once acquired.
